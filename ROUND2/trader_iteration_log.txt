ITERATION 0 — BASELINE (before this log)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Command:
  cd ROUND2 && python -m prosperity4bt trader.py 2 --data bt_data --merge-pnl --no-out --no-progress

Approximate result (from prior session):
  Total PnL ~303,461; merged Sharpe very large (~150+).

Learning — why "Sharpe" looks absurdly high:
  prosperity4bt --merge-pnl reports Sharpe on the MERGED series of *daily*
  PnL totals (here: only 3 days). When three days have similar total profit
  and low day-to-day variance, sample std of [day1, day2, day3] is tiny, so
  mean/std explodes. That is NOT the same as a 252-day annualized equity
  curve Sharpe. Treat it as a diagnostic of consistency across days, not as
  a fund-grade risk metric. Lowering this number by "tuning the bot" is
  misleading; the fix is interpretive (more days, or intraday PnL series).


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ITERATION 1 — OFFLINE DATA ANALYSIS (CSV mids, top-of-book)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Script: load mid = (bid1+ask1)/2 per timestamp from prices_round_2_day_*.csv

Pepper (INTARIAN_PEPPER_ROOT), all three days:
  - Total mid range ~ +1000 per day (strong drift).
  - Per-tick return mean ~ +0.108, std ~ 1.75–2.03.
  - Lag-1 autocorrelation of returns ~ -0.49 to -0.50 (same order as Osmium:
    strong tick-level mean reversion ON TOP of slow drift).

  Time from session open until cumulative mid move exceeds K (ticks):
    K=5  -> ~22–28 ticks
    K=8  -> ~28–86 ticks (day -1 slower early grind)
    K=10 -> ~68–96 ticks

  With correct dual gate (delta < -10 AND fast_ewma < -0.03), no spurious
  SHORT lock in the first 200 ticks on any of the three days.

Learning:
  Pepper is not "pure random walk up"; locally it mean-reverts. Any policy
  that only chases drift without respecting short-horizon MR leaves money
  from buying high / selling low. Osmium-style lag-1 tilt is justified for
  Pepper too, once we are in directional mode.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ITERATION 2 — PEPPER: faster DIRECTION LOCK (cumret threshold)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Change:
  PEPPER_CUMRET_LOCK: 10.0 -> 7.0
  (Still requires fast_drift same sign past PEPPER_FAST_DRIFT_LOCK; warmup
   unchanged.)

Rationale:
  Analysis shows first valid LONG lock happens at ticks 68–96 with threshold
  10; lowering to 7 moves first lock to ~tick 51–71 on day 0/1 and ~71 on day -1,
  recovering tens of ticks of full-size long exposure on a +0.108/tick drift.

Learning (superseded by Iter 3 ablation):
  First implementation bundled cumret=7 with MR=0.35 in one step; net PnL
  dropped vs baseline until ablation separated the two effects (Iter 3).


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ITERATION 3 — PEPPER: MEAN-REVERSION TILT in locked long/short legs
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Change:
  Store pepper_last_ret each tick in _pepper_strategy.
  In _pepper_long_only / _pepper_short_only, shift effective fair by
    -PEPPER_MR_WEIGHT * last_return (lag-1 MR; CSV lag-1 ac ~ -0.49).

  Tried PEPPER_MR_WEIGHT = 0.35.

Backtest (merge-pnl):
  Total PnL: 302,735  (worse than MR-off ablation below)

Ablation (same code path, only constants changed):
  cumret_lock=7,  MR=0.0   -> Total 303,792  Pepper 241,660  sharpe 138.5
  cumret_lock=8,  MR=0.0   -> Total 303,792  (same as 7 on this data: fast_drift
                            gate binds first on some days)
  cumret_lock=10, MR=0.35 -> Total 302,406  (MR hurts when cumret is high)
  cumret_lock=7,  MR=0.2  -> Total 302,997
  cumret_lock=7,  MR=0.5  -> Total 302,992

Learning:
  Pepper has strong lag-1 MR in the MID series, but our *execution* fair is
  microprice + multi-level takes; shading fair by last mid return added
  noise vs edge on the backtester (fills did not improve). The winning knob
  was earlier DIRECTION LOCK (cumret 7) with MR disabled.

  "Lower Sharpe" co-occurred with higher PnL in ablation (138 vs 145) only
  because day-total variance changed slightly — still not economically
  meaningful with n=3 days.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ITERATION 4 — FINAL PICK (ship)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Change:
  PEPPER_CUMRET_LOCK = 7.0
  PEPPER_MR_WEIGHT   = 0.0   (pepper_last_ret kept for future MR experiments)

Backtest (merge-pnl, 3 days):

  Day -1: 100,421  (Osmium 20,401 + Pepper 80,020)
  Day  0: 101,649  (Osmium 21,450 + Pepper 80,199)
  Day  1: 101,722  (Osmium 20,281 + Pepper 81,441)
  Pepper sum: 241,660
  sharpe_ratio: 138.53  (lower than ~145–151 only because 3 daily totals moved;
                         still not a meaningful “risk” number — see Iter 0)
