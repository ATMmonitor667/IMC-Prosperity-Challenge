After thoroughly reviewing the historical trade tape, order book data, and our current codebases (trader.py and traderPrime.py), I have compiled the following insights. Round 1 features two highly distinct assets. Treating them with the same overarching logic will result in heavy drawdowns on at least one side.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ASSET 1: ASH_COATED_OSMIUM (ACO)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Data Profile: Extremely stationary. Over a 3-day span, the price ranges a mere 46 ticks (9,977 to 10,023) and crosses its long-run mean of 10,000 over 4,770 times. 
* Market Microstructure: The average bid-ask spread is ~16 ticks, which is massive compared to its standard deviation (~5.35). Return autocorrelation at lag 1 is strongly negative (-0.495), indicating a textbook bid-ask bounce.
* Strategy Verdict: FIXED-FAIR MARKET MAKING.
  Mean-reversion or momentum here is overthinking it. The best strategy is to become a liquidity provider centered tightly around 10,000. 
  - We should quote passively at `fair - edge` and `fair + edge`. 
  - Given the wide spread, we can comfortably capture edge repeatedly without being run over by trends.
  - Critical implementation detail: Inventory skewing. With an 80-lot limit, we must adjust our quotes based on our current position to avoid getting stuck at max limits and missing out on edge. A micro-price order book imbalance adjustment (as seen in trader.py) is a fantastic touch to avoid adverse selection.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ASSET 2: INTARIAN_PEPPER_ROOT (IPR)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Data Profile: Highly trending and volatile. The asset ranges from 9,998 to 13,007 with a standard deviation of ~866. Most importantly, it only crossed its mean 17 times in 30,000 ticks.
* Market Microstructure: It trends for long stretches. A fixed-fair market maker or naive z-score mean-reversion bot will be absolutely destroyed here as stale quotes are picked off by informed flow.
* Strategy Verdict: TREND FOLLOWING / MOMENTUM WITH ASYMMETRIC QUOTING.
  - We must dynamically calculate the fair value. A dual-EMA (Fast EMA for current regime, Slow EMA for baseline trend) is the optimal way to generate a momentum signal.
  - We shift our fair value in the direction of the momentum. 
  - Quoting must be asymmetric: If the trend is UP, our bid edge should be tight (eager to buy) and our ask edge should be wide (hold onto our longs / reluctant to sell).

CRITIQUE OF CURRENT ALGORITHMS

1. traderPrime.py:
   This is a solid, simplified baseline but it leaves money on the table. It treats IPR with a single EMA, which acts as a lagging indicator rather than a momentum signal. It also uses a static edge for both assets.

   - For IPR, it implements the Dual-EMA beautifully, calculating momentum to shift fair value and applying asymmetric edges. 

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FINAL RECOMMENDATIONS FOR MAXIMIZING PNL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To push our PnL to the absolute maximum, we should adopt the core logic of `trader.py` with the following enhancements:

1. Multi-level Quoting (Laddering): Instead of placing all our available buy/sell capacity at a single price level, we should split our orders across multiple tiers (e.g., size 40 at edge 5, size 25 at edge 8, size 15 at edge 12). This allows us to capture higher spreads during sudden volatility spikes.
2. Volatility-adjusted Edge for IPR: Tie the PEPPER_EDGE not just to momentum direction, but to short-term volatility. If the spread temporarily widens in the tape, our base edge should expand accordingly to protect us from getting picked off.
3. Zero-Cross Hysteresis: Ensure that our momentum signal for IPR has a "dead-zone" around zero. This prevents the bot from rapidly flipping its asymmetric quotes during periods of sideways noise.

By combining `trader.py`'s existing logic with volume laddering, we will dominate the Round 1 order books.
