    snaps back.  The spread is 16 wide.  Whoever captures that

-----------------------------------------------------------------
  ASH_COATED_OSMIUM — deep dive
-----------------------------------------------------------------

Statistical signature:
  - Stationary around 10,000 (mean drift across 3 days = 0.00004/tick)
  - 94.4% of ticks are within ±10 of 10,000
  - 70.3% within ±5.  36.5% within ±2.
  - Lag-1 mid-change autocorr = -0.495.  That is bid/ask bounce:
    prices oscillate between bid and ask tick-to-tick.  This is
    NOT noise — it's structural, and it is EXACTLY what a market
    maker monetises by sitting on both sides.
  - The spread is mostly 16 (with some clusters at 18-19 and
    occasional 6-13 when someone aggresses).  A 16-tick spread
    is enormous on a 10,000-level price — a half-spread of 8 is
    ~8 bps per round trip.  You should be trading this aggressively.

Implication for fair value:
  OSMIUM_FAIR = 10,000 (HARD-CODED, CONSTANT) is correct.
  Do NOT use a rolling mean, EMA, or microprice-as-primary.
  The data mean is 10,000.20 — call it 10,000.  Anything adaptive
  will be chasing bid/ask bounce and giving back edge.

  Small microprice tilt (±0.5 max) is harmless and marginally
  helpful for queue priority, as trader.py already does.

Toy backtests (run against the raw CSVs):
  - z>1 mean-reversion on in-sample mean: +14,343 PnL, 1317 trades
  - momentum (sign of 20-step return):     +31,255 PnL
    ^^^ momentum "wins" only because it piggybacks on
        mean-reversion at short horizons — NOT because there is
        a real trend.  Don't be fooled.
  - naive MM with edge=1, SMA-50 fair:     +1,772 PnL (weak)
        This under-performs because SMA-50 drifts with bid/ask
        bounce.  A FIXED fair at 10,000 blows this out of the
        water.

RECOMMENDED STRATEGY: Aggressive fixed-fair market maker
  fair             = 10,000  (constant)
  edge             = 1        (post at 9999 / 10001)
  skew_divisor     = 20-30    (gently flatten when inventory builds)
  microprice tilt  = optional, ±0.5 max

  Two-phase loop per tick:
    1. TAKE: sweep every ask < fair (buy it), sweep every bid >
       fair (sell it).  This alone yields ~5% of ticks in free
       edge against a deterministic anchor.
    2. MAKE: post a full-size bid at fair-edge-skew and ask at
       fair+edge-skew for the remaining capacity.

  Sanity guards (all present in trader.py):
    - never cross the book (post_bid < best_ask, post_ask > best_bid)
    - if edges collapse, fall back to fair±1

  What's wrong with traderPrime.py here:
    OSMIUM_EDGE = 7 posts at 9993 / 10007.  Given the book is
    nearly always 9992 / 10008 with spread=16, those quotes sit
    INSIDE the book and get almost no fills at the edge you want.
    You essentially only earn the sweep PnL from the ~5% of free-
    take ticks.  Cut edge to 1-2.  trader.py (edge=1) is right.

  What to IMPROVE over trader.py:
    - Consider edge=2 if edge=1 leads to adverse selection on
      ticks where the book is wider than 16; the deeper book
      (9997 bid, 10003 ask on the 2nd level) says this is rare.
      Stick with edge=1; monitor fill ratio.
    - Skew denominator could be adaptive: when abs(position)>60,
      pull the distant quote 1 tick further to accelerate flatten.
    - Use *per-level* book vol when sweeping (already done).


-----------------------------------------------------------------
  INTARIAN_PEPPER_ROOT — deep dive
-----------------------------------------------------------------

Statistical signature (this is where the current code leaves
money on the table):

  Daily trajectory:
    Day -2:  9998.5 -> 11001.5   (+1003.0)
    Day -1: 10998.5 -> 11998.0   (+ 999.5)
    Day  0: 11998.5 -> 13000.0   (+1001.5)

  The drift is LITERALLY constant: slope = 0.01 price units per
  timestamp, every day, to four-decimal precision.  This is NOT
  random — it's a planted linear trend.

  After subtracting the linear trend, what's left?
    residual std          =  2.20
    residual range        =  [-10.7, +10.7]
    residual zero cross.  = 12,735   <-- reverts constantly
    residual lag-1 autocorr (level changes) = -0.50
  So the RESIDUAL behaves exactly like osmium: tight
  mean-reverting noise around a moving anchor.

  Implication: PEPPER = (linear drift) + (osmium-style wobble).

  The 99% "positive 100-tick return" statistic is the killer.
  There is effectively no 100-tick window where the price is
  DOWN vs 100 ticks ago.  Being systematically long — even with
  ZERO alpha — earns ~3,000 ticks of price appreciation on 80
  units = up to +240,000 of mark-to-market gain over 3 days.

Toy backtests:
  - z>1 mean-reversion on FULL-WINDOW mean: +1,501.5 PnL, 1 trade
        (useless — the mean drifts, so the z-score degenerates)
  - momentum (20-step sign):                +22,771.5 PnL
        (ok, but under-uses the deterministic trend)
  - naive SMA-50 MM, edge=1:               -140,283.0 PnL  <<<<<<
        CATASTROPHIC.  A symmetric market maker with a lagging
        fair value is being systematically run over by the trend.
        This is EXACTLY what traderPrime.py risks doing with its
        PEPPER_ALPHA=0.7 single EMA and symmetric edges.

RECOMMENDED STRATEGY: Trend-aware fair value with asymmetric quoting

  The right fair value is NOT a moving average.  It is an
  explicit *projected* fair:

      fair_trend(t) = anchor_price + 0.01 * (t - anchor_t)

  where (anchor_t, anchor_price) is established by the first few
  ticks of the session (e.g. initialise anchor_price = first mid,
  anchor_t = first timestamp; optionally re-anchor slowly using
  an EMA of the *detrended* residual).

  Then:
    - TAKE anything with ask < fair_trend - small_margin (buy)
    - TAKE anything with bid > fair_trend + small_margin (sell)
    - MAKE asymmetric quotes:
         post_bid = fair_trend - 2      (eager to accumulate long)
         post_ask = fair_trend + 4 to 6 (stingy with longs — the
                                          trend keeps lifting price)
    - BIAS inventory LONG: skew target ~+40 (not 0).  Only flatten
      when position exceeds +70 to protect the limit.

  Why NOT use the dual-EMA in trader.py as-is?
    PEPPER_FAST_ALPHA=0.35, PEPPER_SLOW_ALPHA=0.06 is reasonable
    as a *reactive* momentum detector, but:
      (a) EMAs LAG the true trend of +0.01 per tick.  The fast EMA
          sits systematically BELOW true price in an up-trend and
          you quote a bid that never fills while your ask (above
          the lagging EMA, still below actual future price) gets
          picked off.
      (b) The fair value doesn't know the drift is deterministic
          +0.01/tick.  Bake it in explicitly.

  Why NOT pure trend-following (always max long)?
    Works but ignores noise PnL.  The residual wobbles ±10 —
    there's extra PnL from buying the dips and clipping the
    spread AROUND the trend line.  The combined "trend-tilted
    market maker" captures both.

  Minimum viable upgrade if you want to keep the dual-EMA:
    - Start with fair = mid
    - At each tick add an EXPLICIT linear-drift correction:
        fair += 0.01 * (t - t_start_of_session)
      This replaces lagged-EMA momentum with the real slope
      and is the single biggest PnL lever on pepper.

Spread / liquidity considerations:
  - Avg spread 13, typical 11-14.  So an edge of 2 lands you
    INSIDE the current top of book ~50% of the time, giving
    queue priority without crossing.
  - Top-of-book volume ~11-12.  Your 80-limit is realistic to
    fill against in a few ticks.

-----------------------------------------------------------------
  RISK NOTES / WATCH-OUTS
-----------------------------------------------------------------

  * The +1000/day pepper drift may NOT persist in live Round 1
    data — it is almost certainly a synthetic generator.  If the
    live slope is different or changes sign, the explicit-drift
    fair must be able to re-anchor.  Use a slow EMA of (mid -
    fair_trend) as a drift-adjustment term that wakes up only if
    residual bias persists for, say, 500+ ticks.
  * Position limit is 80 per product — the engine will silently
    drop orders that would breach this.  The sweep phase MUST
    clamp to (LIMIT - position) / (LIMIT + position) as both
    trader.py and traderPrime.py already do.
  * Do NOT place quotes at a tick that crosses the book (already
    handled; keep those sanity checks — they are cheap insurance).
  * No cross-product signal is exploitable:  corr_returns ≈ 0.
    Don't waste code on a pair trade.
