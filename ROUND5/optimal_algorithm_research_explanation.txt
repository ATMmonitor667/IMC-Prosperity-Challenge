Round 5 Optimal Algorithm Research Explanation
=============================================

Purpose
-------

The goal of this research was to move from a broad "try many ideas" scan toward a practical
algorithm that can maximize PnL without breaking the working behavior of the profitable
baseline bot.

The research used two main evidence sources:

1. Historical Round 5 price and trade data from:
   - ROUND5/round5/prices_round_5_day_2.csv
   - ROUND5/round5/prices_round_5_day_3.csv
   - ROUND5/round5/prices_round_5_day_4.csv

   - ROUND5/round5/trades_round_5_day_3.csv
   - ROUND5/round5/trades_round_5_day_4.csv

2. Bot logs:
   - ROUND5/LOGS/570532.json, the profitable baseline run.
   - ROUND5/LOGS/571500/571500.json, the failed run from the previous algorithm attempt.

The main research code is:

High-Level Conclusion
---------------------

The best path is not to replace the profitable core algorithm. The profitable 570532 run made
money because it continuously provided selective two-sided liquidity in strong products while
using relative-value, trend, microprice, and tape-flow signals to bias inventory.

The failed 571500 algorithm mostly failed because it stopped trading. It did not lose a huge
amount by trading bad products. Instead, it removed or over-gated the neutral market-making
behavior that had produced most of the baseline PnL.

Therefore, the optimal next algorithm should:

1. Keep the profitable 570532-style core.
2. Restore neutral two-sided quoting when flat.
3. Keep the original avoid-list discipline.
4. Add only the researched branches that survived walk-forward and robustness tests.
5. Use the researched branches as isolated product-specific or pair-specific overlays, not as a
   full replacement for the core.

Research Methodology
--------------------

The first scanner, family_strategy_scan.py, tested broad families of ideas:

1. Microstructure scan
   - Measured spreads, depths, return volatility, and trade counts.
   - Purpose: identify which families have enough liquidity and movement to support trading.

2. Cross-sectional signals
   - Tested family-relative momentum and mean reversion.
   - Looked at whether products that were rich or cheap relative to their family tended to
     continue moving or revert.

3. Residual pair trading
   - Picked pairs inside each family.
   - Fit a beta on one day and traded residual z-scores on the next day.
   - Used walk-forward folds:
     - fit day 2, test day 3
     - fit day 3, test day 4

4. Leader-follower diagnostics
   - Tested whether one product's move predicted another product's future move.

5. Passive fair-value quoting
   - For one target product, estimated fair value from the rest of its family.
   - Quoted passively when the model fair value was sufficiently above or below the market mid.
   - Used public trade tape as a conservative fill proxy.

The second research script, optimal_algorithm_research.py, made this more rigorous by focusing
only on promising candidates and stress-testing them:

1. Passive branch parameter robustness
   - Tested edge thresholds from 0.25 to 1.00 spreads.
   - Tested inventory skew from 0.00 to 1.00 spreads.
   - Tested multiple fair-value model choices where relevant.
   - Recorded worst fold, mean fold, positive folds, fill counts, drawdown, and quarter
     stability.

2. Passive fill stress
   - Tested whether the strategy still filled when requiring public trades to go through the
     quote by 0.25 or 0.50 spreads.
   - Important note: this is a queue/fill-availability stress. In Round 5, many public fills
     happen at the touch, so requiring fill-through often produces zero fills. That does not
     automatically invalidate the base passive strategy.

3. Passive signed markouts
   - For each simulated fill, measured signed markout after 1, 5, 10, 20, and 50 bars.
   - Positive signed markout means buys tended to be followed by higher mids and sells tended
     to be followed by lower mids.

4. Cap sensitivity
   - Tested smaller and larger caps to see whether a branch depended on excessive size.

5. Pair robustness
   - Tested lookbacks of 60, 90, 120, 180, and 300 bars.
   - Tested entry z-scores of 2.0, 2.5, and 3.0.
   - Tested exit z-scores of 0.5 and 1.0.
   - Stress-tested slippage of 0.0, 0.5, and 1.0.

6. Current bot log diagnostics
   - Parsed 570532 and 571500 logs by product and family.
   - Compared final PnL, drawdown, and open positions.
   - Identified whether a product was losing money or simply not being traded.

Why 571500 Failed
-----------------

The failed 571500 run made only 480.15 PnL. The profitable 570532 run made 29845.66 PnL.

The key observation from parsing both logs was:

- 570532 ended with 22 requested-family open positions.
- 571500 ended with only 1 requested-family open position.
- Most products that made money in 570532 had exactly 0 PnL in 571500.

This means the main failure was inactivity.

Major products that disappeared:

- PANEL_4X4:
  - 570532 PnL: 3158.95
  - 571500 PnL: 161.00
  - Delta: -2997.95

- TRANSLATOR_ECLIPSE_CHARCOAL:
  - 570532 PnL: 2996.02
  - 571500 PnL: 0.00
  - Delta: -2996.02

- SLEEP_POD_NYLON:
  - 570532 PnL: 1706.42
  - 571500 PnL: -148.00
  - Delta: -1854.42

- GALAXY_SOUNDS_BLACK_HOLES:
  - 570532 PnL: 1574.77
  - 571500 PnL: 0.00
  - Delta: -1574.77

- UV_VISOR_RED:
  - 570532 PnL: 1574.60
  - 571500 PnL: 0.00
  - Delta: -1574.60

- TRANSLATOR_GRAPHITE_MIST:
  - 570532 PnL: 1183.59
  - 571500 PnL: 0.00
  - Delta: -1183.59

- PANEL_2X4:
  - 570532 PnL: 1081.66
  - 571500 PnL: 0.00
  - Delta: -1081.66

The concrete code-level problems were:

1. Over-gating neutral market making
   - In the failed algorithm, if alpha was small, the state-adjustment logic set repair_only.
   - If the position was zero, repair_only suppressed both buying and selling.
   - This meant the bot often did not quote at all.
   - The profitable 570532 baseline relied on neutral two-sided quoting even when alpha was
     small.

2. Avoid-list behavior was diluted
   - The previous patch tried to let avoided products earn size back.
   - But the profitable baseline treated avoid-list products as truly avoided.
   - Some avoided products were allowed to quote accidentally or inconsistently.

3. Zero-cap products could still create orders
   - The previous algorithm did not return early from _make_orders when cap <= 0.
   - This allowed products that should have been inactive to still place quotes.

4. The algorithm used early research parameters, not the final robustness-selected ones
   - PANEL_2X2 used edge 0.25 and skew 0.75, but final research selected edge 1.00 and
     skew 1.00.
   - UV_VISOR_MAGENTA used rolling regression, but final research selected static day-3
     leave-one-out regression.
   - Pair branches used lookback 90, but the robustness scan favored longer lookbacks.


How The Final Trader Was Changed
--------------------------------

The repaired final_trader.py uses these principles:

1. Keep the profitable core active
   - The bot now restores neutral two-sided quoting when flat.
   - Small alpha does not automatically suppress both sides.

2. Keep avoided products truly avoided
   - If a product is in the avoid list, the generic engine will not trade it.
   - Exceptions are allowed only for explicit researched branches, such as PANEL_2X2 passive
     geometry or residual pair products.

3. Add hard early return for zero cap
   - If the product cap is zero, _make_orders returns flattening orders only.
   - This prevents accidental quotes in disabled products.

4. Add researched passive branches
   - PANEL_2X2:
     - panel_geometry
     - edge 1.00
     - skew 1.00
     - cap 6
     - exclusive branch

   - UV_VISOR_MAGENTA:
     - static_regression
     - edge 0.50
     - skew 1.00
     - cap 5
     - exclusive branch

   - SNACKPACK_PISTACHIO:
     - rolling_regression
     - edge 0.25
     - skew 0.75
     - cap 4
     - exclusive branch

   - GALAXY_SOUNDS_SOLAR_FLAMES:
     - static_regression
     - edge 0.25
     - skew 0.00
     - cap 3
     - non-exclusive, small overlay

5. Add static regression coefficients
   - UV_VISOR_MAGENTA and GALAXY_SOUNDS_SOLAR_FLAMES use hardcoded day-3 trained static
     regression coefficients from the research script.
   - This avoids long warm-up delays and matches the robustness-selected model.

6. Add pair overlays carefully
   - Pair branches do not force large taker trades.
   - They modify alpha/reservation behavior in small sizes.
   - Practical lookbacks were capped at 180 in implementation to control runtime and state size.

7. Control runtime
   - traderData serialization was changed from jsonpickle to compact standard json.
   - Retained histories and pending event queues were capped.
   - Synthetic day-4 validation showed traderData remained under about 283k characters.

Final Algorithmic Recommendation
--------------------------------

The best current algorithm is a hybrid:

1. Use 570532-style core behavior as the base:
   - family-relative value
   - own-product mean reversion
   - selective trend
   - microprice/imbalance
   - anonymous tape-flow
   - passive market making
   - conservative taker logic

2. Preserve the avoid list from the profitable baseline.

3. Add researched passive branches:
   - full size:
     - UV_VISOR_MAGENTA
     - PANEL_2X2
     - SNACKPACK_PISTACHIO
   - small size:
     - GALAXY_SOUNDS_SOLAR_FLAMES

4. Add pair overlays at small strength:
   - SLEEP_POD_POLYESTER vs SLEEP_POD_NYLON
   - MICROCHIP_CIRCLE vs MICROCHIP_RECTANGLE
   - TRANSLATOR_ECLIPSE_CHARCOAL vs TRANSLATOR_VOID_BLUE

5. Do not let researched branches shut down the core.
   - The most important lesson from 571500 is that protecting against bad trades can become
     worse than bad trades if it suppresses the profitable market-making engine.


Remaining Risks
---------------

1. Passive fill modeling is approximate
   - The research used public trade-through checks.
   - Actual queue priority can differ.

2. Pair branch fragility
   - Pair branches showed positive PnL, but some had weak parameter robustness.
   - They should remain small overlays until live logs prove they help.

3. Static coefficients are day-3 trained
   - They worked well in the day3 -> day4 fold.
   - If the hidden environment changes materially, static coefficients could become stale.
   - This is why only the strongest static branches were used.

4. Core still dominates PnL
   - The research branches can improve the algorithm, but the baseline engine still produces
     most of the expected PnL.
   - Any future change must avoid repeating the 571500 failure mode of suppressing core
     liquidity provision.


Bottom Line
-----------

The research does not support replacing the bot with a completely new strategy. It supports a
careful hybrid algorithm:

- keep the profitable 570532 core;
- fix the over-gating that caused 571500 to stop trading;
- add the strongest passive fair-value branches;
- add fragile pair edges only as small overlays;
- keep runtime and traderData size controlled.

That is the logic now implemented in ROUND5/final_trader.py.
